Global Search for Seamless Product Discovery

Achieved a 30% improvement in product discovery and buyflow by designing and rolling out Global Search for Apptium's Cloud Commerce Platform.

A unified search experience that enables resellers to find products, customers, and orders faster—improving sales velocity and operational efficiency.

Role: Product Designer Company: Apptium Timeline: Nov 2024 – Apr 2025 Tools: Figma, Google Analytics, UserTesting, Miro
View Prototype

Overview

Apptium's Cloud Commerce Platform enables resellers to manage, purchase, and sell cloud products. As the platform scaled, resellers struggled to locate products, subscriptions, and customers efficiently. The absence of a unified Global Search fragmented workflows and slowed critical tasks.

My Contribution

Led Global Search UX end-to-end: research synthesis, information architecture, interaction design, prototyping, usability testing, and iterative refinement with engineering.

Problem

How might we help resellers quickly find products, orders, and customer details without friction—enhancing their ability to manage cloud operations efficiently?

Constraints

  • Tight timelines for regional launches
  • Alignment with existing platform architecture
  • Large and rapidly growing datasets

Research & Insights

Mixed-method research combining platform analytics, search logs, time-on-task metrics, and interviews with 15 active APAC resellers (new + experienced).

Insight 1

30% of users instinctively searched for a search bar immediately upon login

Insight 2

Navigation-heavy discovery caused task abandonment as catalogs scaled

Insight 3

Users valued relevant suggestions upfront more than exhaustive result lists

Design Strategy

Optimize for speed-to-relevance, not completeness

Use progressive disclosure to minimize cognitive load

Balance familiar search patterns with contextual enhancements

Hypotheses & Testing

Hypotheses

HMW 1: A tabbed Global Search with progressive disclosure enables efficient cross-module exploration

HMW 2: Showing high-relevance results upfront improves comprehension and task completion

Prototypes

  • Version 1: Deep tab-based drill-down
  • Version 2: Suggestion-first with contextual relevance

Outcome

Version 2 performed better—users completed tasks faster with fewer clicks and greater confidence.

Iterations — Filter Placement & Cognitive Load

Early designs placed filters directly inside the search dropdown to keep users "in flow." Testing showed this increased cognitive load and reduced discoverability—users focused on results and missed refinement controls.

Iteration 1 — Filters Embedded in Dropdown (High Control, High Load)

  • Intent: Allow refinement without leaving search context
  • Observed behavior: Users scanned results first and overlooked filters
  • Issue: Too many competing controls increased cognitive effort

Iteration 2 — Externalized Filters with Lightweight Tags

  • Change: Moved advanced filters outside dropdown
  • Why it worked: Clear separation between results and refinement
  • Result: Faster scanning and intentional narrowing

Design Decision

Progressive disclosure only works when the next step is obvious. In search, results must dominate—controls should support, not compete.

Final Iteration — Scalable Search Experience

Final design — Externalized filters with grouped, cross-module results.

The final iteration evolved Global Search into a structured, multi-column results experience grouped by Marketplace, Subscriptions, and Customers. Persistent refinement controls and visible search criteria improved orientation, confidence, and scalability.

What Changed

  • Results grouped by domain for fast cross-module scanning
  • Filters contextualized by module
  • Search criteria chips showed visible state and easy reset

Why It Worked

  • Reduced cognitive load
  • Scaled with growing catalogs and data
  • Enabled broad scan → focused refinement behavior

Outcome

This structure significantly improved comprehension and refinement speed, contributing directly to a 30% improvement in product discovery time.

Final Reflection

This project reinforced that search is foundational to user trust and platform efficiency. By grounding decisions in behavioral data and disciplined iteration, we delivered a scalable solution that meaningfully improved discovery and purchase flows.
