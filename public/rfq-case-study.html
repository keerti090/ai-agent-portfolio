Introduction

Request for Quote workflows are foundational to B2B software procurement, particularly for enterprise products where pricing cannot be standardized. Products like IBM licenses, Oracle databases, and specialized ISV solutions require negotiated pricing based on volume, relationship tier, and competitive positioning.

Before this project, Apptium's Cloud Commerce Platform handled these negotiations entirely offline through email chains, spreadsheets, and manual approval workflows. This created friction for customers who expected transparent status updates, resellers who lacked visibility into approval timelines, and ISVs who struggled to enforce margin policies consistently.

The offline process also introduced business risk. Pricing discussions happened in unstructured channels, making it difficult to audit decisions, track SLA compliance, or scale the process as transaction volume increased. The goal was to bring this critical workflow into the platform with the appropriate structure, transparency, and governance controls.

Business Context & Ecosystem

The RFQ workflow operates within a three-tier ecosystem:

  • End Customers purchase software licenses through the platform and may request special pricing when standard rates don't align with their volume or use case.
  • Resellers own the customer relationship and have the authority to adjust pricing within predefined margin limits set by the ISV.
  • ISVs (Independent Software Vendors, such as TD SYNNEX) own the product catalog and define margin policies. When a reseller's price adjustment exceeds their approved threshold, the request escalates to the ISV for approval.

This hierarchy ensures pricing control stays with the ISV while giving resellers flexibility to close deals quickly. The challenge was digitizing this approval chain without introducing friction or losing the governance benefits.

Problem Statement

The offline RFQ process created three critical problems:

  • No visibility. Customers couldn't track quote status. Resellers didn't know if their request was pending with the ISV. ISVs lacked a centralized view of all open requests.
  • Inconsistent approval times. Quotes took anywhere from 2 days to 2 weeks depending on email response times and escalation paths. This unpredictability hurt deal velocity.
  • Audit and compliance gaps. Pricing decisions happened in email threads without structured records, making it difficult to enforce policies or review historical decisions.
How might we bring RFQ workflows into the platform in a way that preserves approval hierarchies, improves transparency, and reduces turnaround time without sacrificing governance?

Discovery & Research

The discovery phase focused on understanding the existing offline workflow and identifying pain points across all three personas.

Research Methods

  • Stakeholder interviews with resellers, ISV account managers, and end customers who had recently requested custom pricing
  • Workflow audits to map the current email and spreadsheet-based process
  • Support ticket analysis to identify the most common pricing-related issues

Key Findings

  • Offline workflows introduced an average delay of 5‚Äì7 business days per quote, with some requests taking up to 14 days.
  • Customers had no visibility into quote status and often submitted duplicate requests or escalated to support unnecessarily.
  • Resellers spent significant time chasing ISV approvals via email and Slack, which slowed deal cycles.
  • ISVs lacked a unified view of pending requests, making it difficult to prioritize high-value deals or identify patterns in margin exceptions.
  • Manual escalation paths were inconsistent. Some resellers knew which ISV contact to reach out to; others didn't, leading to uneven approval times.

End-to-End RFQ Workflow

The final workflow digitizes the entire approval chain while maintaining the appropriate governance controls at each step.

  1. Customer submits RFQ ‚Äì The customer selects products, specifies volume, and provides a brief justification for custom pricing.
  2. Reseller reviews and adjusts pricing ‚Äì The reseller receives the request and can approve it immediately if the price adjustment falls within their margin limits.
  3. Escalation to ISV (if needed) ‚Äì If the reseller's adjustment exceeds their approved margin, the request automatically escalates to the ISV for review.
  4. ISV approval or revision ‚Äì The ISV can approve the request, reject it with a reason, or suggest alternative pricing.
  5. Customer notified ‚Äì The customer receives an in-platform notification and email with the final decision and next steps.
  6. Quote converts to checkout ‚Äì If approved, the customer can proceed directly to checkout with the custom pricing locked in.

The Solution: Automated RFQ Workflow

How the solution works:

  • Customer submits RFQ ‚Üí System captures context (volume, timeline, justification)
  • Auto-routes to reseller ‚Üí Margin limits displayed upfront, instant approval if within threshold
  • Intelligent escalation ‚Üí Automatically routes to ISV with full context if margin exceeded
  • Transparent tracking ‚Üí All parties see real-time status; email notifications at state changes
  • Audit trail ‚Üí Every decision logged with timestamp and reason

UX Design by Persona

Customer Experience

The customer-facing RFQ flow starts from the product detail page or shopping cart. When a customer requests custom pricing, they provide context about their volume, use case, and timeline. This information helps resellers and ISVs make informed decisions quickly.

Once submitted, the customer can track quote status from their account dashboard. Status updates include:

  • Pending review by reseller
  • Under ISV review (if escalated)
  • Approved with final pricing
  • Rejected with reason

Email notifications are sent at each state change to keep customers informed without requiring them to check the platform constantly.

Reseller Experience

Resellers access all pending RFQs from a dedicated queue in their admin panel. Each request shows the customer's context, the requested discount, and the margin impact.

The system displays the reseller's margin limits in real time, so they know immediately whether they can approve the request or need to escalate to the ISV. If escalation is required, the reseller adds internal notes to provide context for the ISV reviewer.

Resellers can also batch-process similar requests and set up notification preferences to avoid inbox overload.

ISV Experience

ISVs can approve, reject, or suggest alternative pricing. All decisions are logged with timestamps and reasons, creating an auditable record for compliance and policy enforcement.

The ISV dashboard also surfaces trends such as the most common margin exceptions, high-volume resellers, and seasonal pricing patterns, helping ISVs refine their policies over time.

Design Iterations & Key Decisions

Early Assumptions

The initial design assumed customers would want to negotiate pricing through a chat-like interface. Early prototypes included a messaging thread where customers, resellers, and ISVs could discuss pricing back and forth.

What Didn't Work

User testing revealed that the chat interface added unnecessary complexity. Customers didn't want to negotiate‚Äîthey wanted clarity on whether their request was approved. Resellers found the unstructured conversation difficult to manage at scale. ISVs needed structured data, not open-ended discussions.

How the Design Evolved

The final design shifted to a structured, state-based workflow. Each request moves through predefined states with clear decision points. Internal notes are still available for resellers and ISVs, but the customer-facing experience is simplified to status updates and final outcomes.

Key Design Decisions

  • Margin visibility: Show resellers their margin limits upfront so they understand approval authority before submitting a request to the ISV.
  • Contextual information: Surface relevant data at each step, such as the customer's purchase history, the product's standard margin policy, and recent similar approvals.
  • Automated escalation: Remove manual escalation paths. If a reseller exceeds their margin limit, the system automatically routes the request to the ISV.
  • Audit trail: Log all decisions, timestamps, and reasons to ensure compliance and enable retrospective analysis.

KPI Dashboard & Operational Visibility

A critical component of the RFQ system is the operational dashboard for ISVs and platform administrators. The dashboard tracks key metrics that indicate system health and identify bottlenecks.

Tracked KPIs

  • Quote volume: Total RFQs submitted, broken down by product category and reseller
  • Approval time: Median time from submission to final decision, segmented by approval path (reseller-only vs. ISV escalation)
  • Win rate: Percentage of approved quotes that convert to completed purchases
  • Bottlenecks: Identification of requests stuck in pending states for longer than the target SLA

The dashboard enables ISVs to optimize their approval policies based on real data. For example, if a particular product category consistently requires ISV approval, the ISV can adjust the reseller's margin limits to enable faster self-service approvals.

Impact & Outcomes

The RFQ workflow launched in October 2025 and immediately improved operational efficiency across all three personas.

Quantitative Outcomes

  • 40% reduction in turnaround time: Average quote resolution dropped from 5‚Äì7 business days to 3‚Äì4 business days, measured via system timestamps.
  • ~35% increase in quote-to-conversion rate: Transparency in the request process and faster turnaround times led to more quotes converting to completed purchases, as customers experienced less friction and uncertainty during the approval process.
  • Improved deal velocity for enterprise SKUs: High-value deals with complex pricing requirements moved through the approval process faster, reducing the risk of deals stalling during procurement.

Qualitative Feedback

Resellers reported that the structured workflow reduced manual coordination and allowed them to focus on customer relationships rather than chasing approvals. ISVs valued the audit trail and the ability to track margin trends over time. Customers appreciated the transparency and predictability of the new process.

Final Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of designing for governance, not just the happy path. In B2B systems, workflows need to balance user efficiency with business controls. The most effective designs make compliance invisible‚Äîusers follow the right process without feeling constrained.

One of the most valuable lessons was understanding that transparency reduces support burden. When users can see the status of their request and understand why decisions are made, they're less likely to escalate unnecessarily. This principle applies beyond RFQ workflows to any multi-stakeholder approval process.

The approach taken here‚Äîstructured states, automated escalation, and contextual information at each decision point‚Äîcan scale to other enterprise workflows such as contract approvals, discount authorizations, and compliance reviews. The key is identifying the approval hierarchy, surfacing the right data at the right time, and creating an auditable record without adding friction.
